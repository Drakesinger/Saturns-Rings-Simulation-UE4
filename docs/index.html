Saturn's Rings

A visual simulation made in Unreal Engine 4
By Horia Mut

About

Saturn and its rings

A prototype created for a swiss museum's VR exhibition whose goal is to instruct people on the great distances that separate the astral bodies within our solar system. The objectives of the protoype are two-fold: showcase a visually stunning and realistic depiction of Saturn, and be able to travel into its rings and see the particles that compose it. The final product will have participants don a VR headset and go on a trip throughout our solar system, in a highly advanced spaceship. They will be able to see the mountains and valleys of Mars, the gases of Jupiter, and go through the rings of Saturn.

This prototype has been developed during my Master's Degree Deepening Project (Projet d'Approfondissement) at HES-SO. Proposed by Prof. Stéphane Gobron, this is one of the first Unreal Engine projects of HE-Arc-Ingénierie. I was asked to create a realistic experience for a stop of the previously mentioned space trip, and to provide two training sessions in Unreal Engine 4 to the Image Processing & Computer Graphics group along with two more introductory sessions to the current class of Game Technologies.

Features

Scale 1:10000

The scene is at a scale of 1 centimeter (Unreal's default unit) is 1 km. All distances are real; i.e.,each ring is at the correct distance from Saturn's equator and has the correct, known, radius.

Level Of Detail (LOD)
transitions

You are able to navigate through space, at a default speed of 300 km per second, and go as close to Saturn and the rings as you wish. Transitions are seamless, without any loading screens. One thing missing: collisions. Assume you are using cheat-mode and Obi-Wan is helping you. Click the image for a video.

50'000+ moving astral bodies

All particles forming the rings have orbital and local rotations. They orbit around the planet at various speeds, and have their own rotations along random axis.

A song of Fire and Ice

The contents of Saturn's rings are not really known, and we do not know how the particles forming them look like. We know most are made of water.

Tech Art Aid's Tutorials

Oskar's tutorial have been a god-send, as Unreal's documentation regarding Hierarchical Instanced Static Meshes (HISM) was pretty sparse at that time. Everything within the profiling tutorials is pure gold and a must-see for any Unreal Engine developer.

Planetarium Planet Blueprint

The Planetarium assets were used in the beginning of the project, as a starting point for how the planet will look and for its skybox. We have modified the skybox to better suit our needs, changed its lighting to dynamic, and used a different texture for the MilkyWay.

Controls

The following controls are available regardless of the platform:

  • WASD for directonal movement.
  • QE to roll.
  • RF to change altitude (up/down).
  • Mouse-wheel to change movement speed.
  • CTRL+H to stop orbital rotation of all asteroids.
  • ESC to quit the application.

Oculus-Rift Touch Controls

Controls have been set up for Oculus Touch as the documentation for the Vive inputs was nowhere to be found.

  • Right Thumbstick for directional movement (forward/backward, right/left)
  • Left Thumbstick to roll and change altitude.
  • Press X to reset your view.
  • Press the Y button to stop the orbital rotation of asteroids.
  • You can't change your speed in VR with the controllers.
